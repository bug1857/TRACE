01 / 08Problem Understanding
Supply Chains Are a Black Box
Enterprises have zero real-time visibility into emissions, violations, and process drift
  • No process visibility: Companies cannot trace actual logistics sequences against intended process models — violations go undetected for months
  • Manual carbon accounting: Scope 1/2/3 emissions estimated quarterly with spreadsheets — not per-shipment, not per-activity
  • Regulation blindness: BRSR (India), GHG Protocol, and ESG frameworks require detailed traceability that no existing tool provides end-to-end
  • Supplier opacity: No system ranks supplier carbon fitness per transaction or flags which partners drive the most violations

Real Cost

Supply chain emissions account for up to 80% of a company's total carbon footprint — yet most enterprises can only estimate them annually at best

Current State

ERP logs sit unused. Process analysts export CSVs manually. Carbon consultants bill by the hour to do what algorithms should do in seconds.

What's Needed

An automated platform that ingests raw event logs, detects process violations, computes carbon per activity, and generates regulator-ready reports — instantly.

The Gap

Existing tools (ProM, Celonis) do process mining or carbon accounting. None do both. None are accessible without expensive consultants.
